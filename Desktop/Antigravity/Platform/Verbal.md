| **1BTC Question 1**  Although the committee initially regarded the proposal as overly cautious, subsequent developments suggested that such restraint was not only justified but \_\_\_\_\_\_.   salutary innovative obligatory perfunctory   obstructive  (difficulty: 4/5)  **Explanation:** Although sets up a contrast with “initially …overly cautious” so we know that the proposal must turn out to be something other than that. This could be “the right amount of caution” or “not cautious enough”. The second part of the sentence, “such restraint was not only justified but also \_\_\_\_\_\_\_” shows that our target needs to describe the  “restraint” as **something even better than / beyond “justified”** *Salutary* means producing good effects; beneficial. Not only reasonable but actually beneficial/ a good idea. Worth keeping. *Innovative* is a positive term that fits the correct general idea of something positive, but it isn’t justified. It doesn’t pair well with “cautious” and “restraint”, and nothing in the passage indicates that it would be innovative. Not our answer. *Obligatory* means required; this is a very tempting choice. The sentiment that would be expressed by a phrase meaning “not only reasonable but required” makes sense; it is also a strong fit for the first indication that the proposal was  “not overly cautious”. Keep it. *Perfunctory* (done with minimal effort) and *obstructive* (causing blockages) make no sense; in any case, these are both negative terms and we’re looking for something positive / even better than justified to contrast the negative initial sentiment.  The best two answers are *salutary* and *obligatory*. But with *obligatory,* a closer look at the definition shows why it isn’t quite correct.  If it just meant “necessary” or “required *by the situation*” it could be our choice, but *obligatory* actually means something required by a legal, moral, or other rule. A closer synonym is compulsory.  It doesn’t make sense to find out in the end that such restraint is required by law; it makes more sense that such restraint turned out to be a good idea / beneficial.  |
